Research

Temperature Dependent SiC MOSFETs Characterization

A novel method to characterize the temperature dependent switching loss in SiC MOSFET was proposed which will enable power electronics designers to accurately estimate the losses for a target ambient temperature, which in turn will help them to optimize the thermal design. Given a converter with SiC based half bridge configuration, the proposed method can be used to characterize the reverse recovery current of the body diode, which has been shown to be the dominant factor for the temperature dependence.

On-Board Vehicle Charger

A 1.5 kW, 220/230 V, 50 Hz (AC) and 300-400 V (DC)SiC-based single-stage current-fed onboard vehicle charger (OBC) 1.5 kW, 220/230 V, 50 Hz (AC) and 300-400 V (DC), adhering to IEEE 519 power quality and UL 2202 safety standards was developed in the lab.An improved modulation technique is developed and implemented to increase light-load efficiency with peak efficiency of 94%.

Wireless Power Transfer

A 1kW Type-1 wireless power transfer(WPT) based charging solution was developed for 72V battery subsystems prevalent in domestic markets.The sytem also uses proprietary dual phase shift control algorithms to reduce the number of controllers less than that used by commercial solutions.

High Bandwidth Current Sensing

PCB Rogowski Coil based current sensors for Silicon Carbide based MOSFETS were developed for both TO-247 package and 62mm power modules. These sensors achieve bandwidths of upto 100MHz with a fraction of cost of commercially available solutions. The above sensors were used to provide fast current sensing based protection to the above mentioned devices.
